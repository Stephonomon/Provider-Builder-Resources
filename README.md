# Provider Builder Program Resources

A comprehensive reference guide for Epic Clinical Builders — covering everything from choosing a training path and getting certified, to building standards, content migration, clinical decision support governance, and measuring the value of your program.

---

## What's in This Repository

### 📘 [Epic Clinical Builder — Complete Resource Guide](./Epic%20Clinical%20Builder%20-%20Complete%20Resource%20Guide.md)

A single, consolidated markdown document compiled from the CHOP DTS Epic Clinical Builder program knowledge base. The guide is organized into 12 sections spanning the full lifecycle of a clinical builder — from first considering the role through advanced build and program leadership.

| Section | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| **1. Introduction** | The case for clinical builders: why they matter, what the program is designed to accomplish, and the experience and interests that make someone a strong candidate |
| **2. Choosing Your Training Path** | A side-by-side comparison of the two main pathways — full Physician Builder certification vs. the lighter-weight STAR WARS SmartTool track — with a decision guide to help clinicians choose |
| **3. Program Policy and Governance** | Builder tier structure (Younglings through Jedi Masters), security access by tier, general build principles, approval process, Epic environment overview, account setup, and guidance on **measuring the value and impact of your builder program** |
| **4. Getting Started** | New builder onboarding checklist, initial access tasks, and what to do once your builder account is created |
| **5. Training and Certification** | All Epic certification tracks (Physician Builder, Analytics, Healthy Planet, Clinical Content Builder, and specialty tracks), course prerequisites, and step-by-step registration instructions |
| **6. Maintaining Certification** | How Continuing Epic Education (CEE) works — Recommended New Version Training (NVT) and the Application Essentials proctored exam — and what happens if certification lapses |
| **7. Accessing Epic Environments** | Step-by-step login instructions for DEV, DC DEV, TEXT/Reflections (including elevated account setup), SUP, and the Epic Foundation Hosted reference environment |
| **8. Building in Epic: Standards and Style** | Pre-build planning questions, naming conventions, record-keeping requirements, how to retire records, SmartData Element organization, and a full master files reference table by INI |
| **9. Tracking, Submitting, and Migrating Build** | How to use Content Management to track and submit records, the pre-migration checklist, complete vs. partial build migration paths, weekly migration batch process, and what to do if something goes wrong in Production |
| **10. Clinical Decision Support: OPA Standards** | Institutional standards for OurPractice Advisories (OPAs/BPAs) — choosing the right alert type, importance levels, content and language guidelines, audience targeting, trigger design, acknowledgment reasons, governance and CDS Committee review requirements, a full pre-build checklist, and the approved Charms library |
| **11. Advanced Tools: Epic Studio and Lookitt** | Setup and usage of Epic Studio and Lookitt for working with Caché/M routines — including a macro cheatsheet, process management, and the change request process for routine migrations to Production |
| **12. Pathways to Clinical Informatics** | Broader career development resources: graduate degree programs, academic certificates, professional certifications (ACHIP), internal training programs, online learning platforms, AMIA membership, and recommended pathways by career stage |

## ⚠️ Important: CHOP-Specific Content

This guide was originally developed at the **Children's Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP)**. Many references are institution-specific, including:

- Environment URLs
- Internal ticketing systems (ServiceNow)
- Contact names and email addresses
- Organizational names
- Builder tier names ("Younglings," "Padawans," "Jedi Knights," "Jedi Masters")
- Naming convention prefixes (`CHOP`)

**If you are adopting this guide at another organization**, update the following before distributing:

- [ ] Environment and portal URLs
- [ ] Ticketing/service desk references
- [ ] Internal contact names and email addresses
- [ ] Organization prefix in naming conventions
- [ ] Builder tier names and governance body names
- [ ] Analytics/CDW access pathways

The core Epic build concepts, governance frameworks, and clinical decision support standards are broadly applicable across Epic organizations and should require only targeted edits.
